7 Phases of New Product Development

1

Ideation (Idea Generation)

Generate new product ideas from customers, salespeople, and market research. Use divergent thinking to explore possibilities and value co-creation with consumers.

2

Product Concept Development & Screening

Expand ideas into complete product concepts. Screen for technical feasibility and commercial viability. Weed out concepts with little chance of success.

3

Marketing Strategy Development

Identify target market, estimate size, and determine positioning. Plan pricing, distribution, and promotion for both launch and long-term.

4

Business Analysis

Evaluate demand, required resources, and impact on existing products (cannibalization). Determine if product can make profitable contribution.

5

Technical Development

R&D develops physical prototypes. Engineers refine design and production processes. Conduct focus groups and field trials with prospective customers.

6

Market Test

Try complete marketing plan in limited geographic areas. Evaluate and improve before full launch. May use simulated tests to avoid alerting competitors.

7

Commercialization

Full-scale launch with production, distribution, advertising, and sales promotion. May use crowdfunding platforms to fund and validate demand.

5 Factors Affecting Rate of Adoption

Relative Advantage

Does the product provide superior benefits compared to existing options?

Compatibility

Is it consistent with existing values, customs, and practices?

Complexity

How difficult is the product to understand or use?

Trialability

How easy is it to sample the product and its benefits?

Observability

How visible are the product and its benefits to others?
